# care4food-gis
Open GIS repository for the Care4Food project (dashboard and dataset): Behavioral and Territorial Drivers from project choices and perceptions of Food security and Sustainability in a changing climate 

Overview

The Care4Food Dashboard is a collaborative geospatial monitoring platform that visualises food consumption patterns, food environments, and sustainable diet scores across Portugal at NUTS III and municipal levels.

Developed within the project Choices and Perceptions of Food Security and Sustainability in a Changing Climate (Project: FP23-2B073, funded by La Caixa - CALL SOCIAL IMPACT OF CLIMATE CHANGE), the platform combines behavioural survey data with spatial indicators to support evidence-based analysis of food sustainability transitions in Portugal.

The dashboard enables the exploration of territorial differences in dietary practices, food environments, and sustainability performance, supporting researchers, policymakers, municipalities, and citizens.


The Care4Food Dashboard integrates information from multiple sources:

Primary Data
National survey on food habits, food security, and sustainability perceptions conducted in Portugal (2024).
Individual responses aggregated to municipal and NUTS III levels.
Open Data Sources
Directorate-General for Territory (DGT/SNIG)
OpenStreetMap (OSM)
Statistics Portugal (INE)
Sustainable Diet Score resulting from primary data


The Care4Food Sustainable Diet Score is calculated using indicators related to:
Consumption of animal proteins;
Consumption of plant proteins;
Consumption of ultra-processed foods;
Food purchasing behaviour;
Food waste practices.

Individual Sustainable Diet Score are aggregated to produce municipal and regional indicators.


Detailed methodological information is available in the project documentation.
